The Problem

Three Layers of Systemic Failure

Manual procurement evaluation is not merely slow — it is structurally exploitable at every layer. Nyayadarsi addresses all three simultaneously.

Layer 01 · Surface
Operational Inefficiency
A committee evaluating 10 bidders against a 200-page tender spends days cross-checking hundreds of documents in different formats. Manual review is slow, inconsistent across evaluators, and impossible to audit.
01
Layer 02 · Structural
Dual Threat Blindspot
Evaluators face two threats simultaneously — accidental non-compliance from small businesses struggling with complex paperwork, and deliberate obfuscation from bad actors burying fraudulent certificates in blurry scans. Most platforms solve only the first.
02
Layer 03 · Governance
Criteria Manipulation
Tender criteria are sometimes written to include certain bidders and exclude legitimate competition. CAG audit findings confirm this pattern repeatedly across Indian government bodies. Nyayadarsi audits the tender itself — before bidding opens.
03

How It Works

The Nyayadarsi Engine

01
Criteria Engine — Semantic Tender Parsing
Every clause in the tender document is classified into Technical, Financial, or Compliance categories. Each criterion is tagged as a HARD BLOCKER, WEIGHTED FACTOR, or CONTEXTUAL item based on language — "shall" vs "preferred" vs "may". The entire tender converts to a machine-readable JSON master checklist.
{ "criterion_id": "FIN_001", "type": "financial", "description": "Minimum Annual Turnover", "threshold": 50000000, "currency": "INR", "mandatory": true, "blocker": true, "acceptable_evidence": ["CA_certificate", "audited_balance_sheet"], "specificity_score": 0.3 }

Anomalous specificity detected: Equipment criterion references a specific model number (XRF-7200) rather than a performance standard. Only one known vendor manufactures this product. Flag raised for senior procurement officer review before bidding opens.

4-Layer Ingestion Engine — Document Understanding
Every bidder document — digital PDF, scanned image, Word file, or photograph of a physical certificate — is routed through a four-stage pipeline. LayoutLMv3 preserves spatial relationships in tables. Standard OCR destroys table structure; a number without its column header is meaningless. Nyayadarsi preserves that relationship.

Collusion Risk Engine

After all individual evaluations, Nyayadarsi runs cross-bidder anomaly analysis — detecting structural fraud that is invisible to manual review. Five detection flags surface to a senior officer with full evidence. None trigger automatic disqualification.

🏢
Shared Address Flag
Multiple bidders share a registered office address — the common signature of shell company networks simulating competitive bids.
🖊️
CA Fingerprint Flag
Financial documents from multiple bidders originate from the same CA firm with unusual formatting consistency — may indicate coordinated financial statement preparation.
📊
Bid Clustering Flag
Statistical analysis of bid values. Bids clustering within a suspiciously narrow band just above the L1 winner is the classic signature of cartel bidding. Coefficient of variation computed and flagged.
🔗
Ownership Network Flag
Using MCA director data, checks whether a director of the winning firm is linked to the officer who authorised the tender. Conflict of interest detected at evaluation stage.
📄
Document Quality Asymmetry
A specific compliance document with significantly worse scan quality than the rest of the submission — deliberate degradation to frustrate extraction is a documented obfuscation tactic.
